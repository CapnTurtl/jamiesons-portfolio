01 — Who I Am

About Me

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at York University who builds reliable hardware for challenging problems. My work spans autonomous vehicles, nuclear energy infrastructure, and robotics — with a focus on validation engineering, mechatronic design, and making complex systems work in the real world.

Through roles at Zoox, Ontario Power Generation, and Bruce Power, I've developed deep expertise in hardware-in-the-loop testing, failure analysis, and root-cause investigation for safety-critical systems.

At Zoox, I designed and built a portable diagnostic test rig for autonomous vehicle subsystems, teaching myself UDS protocols and automotive communication from scratch to solve a real validation bottleneck. In nuclear, I worked on predictive maintenance for reactor components and ASME pressure vessel integrity — systems where failure isn't an option.

Currently I'm designing suspension systems for Formula SAE and developing humanoid robotic hardware at WATonomous.

"I like building things that work — and proving they'll keep working."
